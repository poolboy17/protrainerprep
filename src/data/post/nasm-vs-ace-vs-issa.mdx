## Curriculum Comparison

### NASM: The Program Design Cert

NASM's OPT Model is the most detailed training framework of the three. It gives you specific protocols for each of five training phases — stabilization, strength endurance, hypertrophy, maximal strength, and power. The corrective exercise content and assessment protocols are also best-in-class.

**Weakest area:** Nutrition (surface-level) and business skills (barely covered). NASM would rather you buy those as separate certifications.

### ACE: The People Skills Cert

ACE's behavior-change coverage is unmatched. Motivational interviewing, stages of change, rapport building, client communication — ACE dedicates meaningful curriculum time to the skills that actually keep clients coming back. The IFT Model is a solid training framework, though less prescriptive than NASM's OPT.

**Weakest area:** Program design depth. The IFT Model provides guidelines where the OPT Model provides prescriptions.

### ISSA: The Flexibility Cert

ISSA's curriculum is the most broadly scoped of the three, covering personal training, basic nutrition, and business fundamentals in one package. The open-book exam format and self-paced study make it the most flexible option. ISSA also offers aggressive bundle deals (CPT + Nutrition + another cert for one price).

**Weakest area:** Perceived rigor. The open-book exam format causes some employers and industry professionals to view ISSA less seriously, even with NCCA accreditation. This perception is fading but still exists.

## Exam Experience

**NASM** is the hardest exam of the three by most accounts. The 64% pass rate reflects a gap between the study materials and the actual test. Expect to see questions that test application, not just recall — case study questions where you need to identify movement compensations or select appropriate OPT phases.

**ACE** falls in the middle. The 72% pass rate and 3-hour time limit suggest a challenging but manageable exam. Questions emphasize client scenarios and behavior-change applications alongside exercise science.

**ISSA** offers an open-book format with no time limit for its standard exam. This makes it the easiest to pass but also raises the question of what the certification is actually testing. ISSA does offer a separate NCCA-accredited proctored exam option.

## Bundle Deals and Upsells

This is where ISSA stands out. Their bundle pricing is aggressive:

- **ISSA Elite Trainer**: CPT + Nutrition + one specialization for ~$999 total
- **ISSA CPT + Nutrition**: Two certs for the price many charge for one

NASM and ACE both upsell heavily after initial certification. NASM's specialization certs (CES, PES, CNC) run $599–$899 each. ACE's specializations are similarly priced.

If you know you want multiple certifications, ISSA's bundles offer the best per-cert value. If you just want a solid CPT and nothing else, the bundles are irrelevant.

## The Question Nobody Else Asks
